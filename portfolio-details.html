Analyzing Global Tech Layoffs

Data Cleaning & Exploratory Analysis on the Layoffs Dataset

Introduction

This project focuses on cleaning and analyzing global tech layoffs data to uncover key trends, patterns, and insights. Using structured SQL queries, the dataset was cleaned for consistency and completeness, followed by exploratory analysis to identify industry impacts, country-wise layoffs, and company-specific events. The findings aim to support data-driven understanding of workforce shifts and broader economic impacts.



The DataSet

This project uses a dataset containing global tech layoff records from 2020 onward, with details such as company name, industry, location, date, number of employees laid off, and company stage. The raw data was cleaned and prepared to ensure consistency and accuracy before analysis.



Methodology

1. Data Cleaning

Performed thorough data cleaning using SQL queries to prepare the dataset for analysis.

  1. Removed duplicate records to maintain data integrity.
  2. Standardized text fields, including:
    • Trimming extra spaces in the company column.
    • Correcting inconsistent entries in the industry and stage columns.
  3. Converted date columns from text format to proper DATE format for accurate time-based analysis.
  4. Handled missing or blank values to prevent skewed insights.
  5. Dropped unnecessary columns to enhance query performance and reduce memory usage.

2. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Analyzed global layoff trends using SQL to uncover patterns by industry, company, country, and time periods.

Key Insights Uncovered

  • Highest Layoff Events: Identified the company with the highest single-day layoff and the month/year with the largest total layoffs globally. Also found companies that laid off 100% of their workforce, indicating complete shutdowns.
  • Company, Industry & Country Trends: Aggregated layoffs by company, industry, and country. Revealed that Consumer and Retail industries were among the most impacted.
  • Year-wise Trends: Grouped data by year to detect spikes in layoffs, especially during COVID-19 and other downturns.

Advanced Analysis with Window Functions

  • Rolling Totals by Country: Used SUM() OVER() to compute monthly cumulative layoffs for each country.
  • Ranking by Layoff Severity: Applied DENSE_RANK() to extract top 5 companies per year with the most layoffs.
  • Cumulative Layoffs by Industry: Used SUM() OVER(PARTITION BY industry ORDER BY year) to track layoff accumulation over time.
  • Layoff Duration Analysis: Calculated duration in days between the first and last layoff per company using DATEDIFF().
  • Year-over-Year Decrease Detection: Employed LAG() to detect industries showing reduced layoffs over time.

Business-Oriented Questions Answered

  • Which companies and industries were most affected?
  • Are layoffs increasing or decreasing by year?
  • Which funding stages are tied to higher layoffs?
  • Are there signs of recovery in any industries?

Tools & Concepts Applied

  • SQL Concepts: Aggregations (SUM(), AVG(), ROUND(), GROUP BY, ORDER BY), CTEs, Joins, Temporal Functions (YEAR(), SUBSTRING(), DATEDIFF()), Window Functions (ROW_NUMBER(), RANK(), DENSE_RANK(), LAG(), SUM() OVER())
  • Business Thinking: Trend detection, severity analysis, industry performance comparison
  • Database: layoffs_staging2 (cleaned and standardized)

Results

Industries with Largest Year-over-Year Layoff Reductions

Analyzed global layoffs data to identify industries with the most significant decrease in layoffs compared to the previous year, using CTEs and Window Functions like LAG() and RANK().

  • Industries: Transportation, Travel, Real Estate, Food, Retail
  • Year: 2021-2023
  • Trend Insight: Industries like Transportation, Travel, and Real Estate showed significant recovery, with layoffs dropping by over 85% compared to the previous year. This indicates a sector-specific rebound, likely driven by the return of demand post-pandemic.
  • Insight: Transportation had the most dramatic recovery, with a 98.6% reduction in layoffs in 2021 compared to 2020.
  • Interpretation:Economic uncertainty and overhiring during the pandemic may have led to aggressive workforce reductions.

Top Countries with Highest Rolling Layoffs

Analyzed global layoffs dataset to examine which countries experienced the highest cumulative layoffs across the post-pandemic period, using JOINS,CTEs, and Window Functions OVER().

  • Countries: United States, India, Brazil, Netherlands, Sweden
  • Years Analyzed: 2020 - 2023
  • Trend Insight: The peak in rolling layoffs occurred in 2022, with the United States dominating the global total, followed by India. While most countries saw layoffs taper off in 2023, the U.S. still reported over 740K layoffs that year.
  • Insight: The United States experienced over 1.17 million layoffs in 2022 alone — nearly 10x more than India, the second-highest.
  • Interpretation: Post-pandemic correction in the tech and startup ecosystem, combined with global inflation and interest rate hikes, likely triggered mass layoffs across multiple sectors — especially in the U.S., where overexpansion and hiring surges during the pandemic were most aggressive.

Top 5 Companies with Highest Layoffs

Analyzed global layoffs dataset to identify the companies with the most significant number of layoffs in a single year.

  • Companies: Google, Meta, Amazon, Microsoft, Ericsson
  • Year: 2022 - 2023
  • Trend Insight: Majority of layoffs were concentrated in 2023, signaling a peak in post-pandemic tech downsizing.
  • Insight: Google laid off 12,000 employees in 2023 - the highest among all.
  • Interpretation: The concentration of layoffs in 2023 among top tech firms suggests cost-cutting measures in response to slower growth. These companies may have adjusted workforce sizes to align with post-pandemic market demand.

Layoffs Trend by Funding Stages

Analyzed a global layoffs dataset using CTEs and Grouped Aggregations to explore how layoffs varied across companies at different funding stages over time.

  • Funding Stages: Post-IPO, Unknown, Series C, Series D, Acquired, Others
  • Years Analyzed: 2020 - 2023
  • Trend Insight: Layoffs spiked sharply in 2022 across nearly all funding stages, with Post-IPO companies seeing the most significant surge, followed by those in Unknown category and Series C.
  • Insight: Post-IPO companies laid off over 160K employees in 2022 - far exceeding any other category. Despite a slight decline in 2023, this stage remained the most affected, indicating prolonged instability in public markets.
  • Interpretation: The spike in layoffs in 2022 suggests a correction period post-pandemic, with investors tightening funding and companies - especially those at later funding stages - adjusting rapidly to changing market dynamics, rising costs, and overexpansion during 2020–2021.
